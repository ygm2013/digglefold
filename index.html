About us

Our history

Digglefold Primary School buildings originated from a farm that was owned by Major Diggle - from whom the school gets its name. During Second world war the premises were used as a polish refugee camp and were converted to a school in 1948. Since the change from a farm to a school the following addictions were made: 1940 laundry (may have been erected when the premises became a refugee camp), 1943 stables were converted into recreation and ablutions, (the present Winchester House), 1943 Alterations and addictions to Romsey Hostel, 1948 a dinning room was added at the kitchen, 1948 Winchester House alterations were done, 1952 teaching area and toilet block were added, 1960 The sickbay was erected, 1963 the School hall was erected,

Since inception the school has had the following Heads:

  1. Mr S.A.C Pearse (1948 to 1956)
  2. Mr R.H.W McGee (1956 to 1966)
  3. Mr D. Saunders (1966 to 1983)
