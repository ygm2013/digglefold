Digglefold School

Mission statement

To provide high quality formal education for Digglefold School children through the acquisition of self discipline and esteem, (pre-requisite for intellectual development) relevant knowledge, skills and attitudes and the development of good self-reliance skills.

Our history

Digglefold Primary School The complex of buildings now forming the school originated as a farm owned by Major Diggle and from whom the school gets its name. During the war years (second world war) the premises were used as a polish refugee camp and were converted to a school in 1948. During the years since the change from a farm the following addictions were made. 1940 laundry 1943 Conversion of stables into recreation and ablutions.(the present Winchester House) 1943 Alterations and addictions to Romsey Hostel 1948 Dining Room at kitchen 1948 Winchester House alterations 1952 Teaching area and toilet block The sickbay: It was erected in 1960. The School hall: It was erected in 1963. Laundry: It may have been erected in 1940 when the premises became a refugee camp. School Heads 1. Mr S.A.C Pearse 1948 to 1956 2.
